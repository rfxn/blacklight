False-positive adjudicator — evaluates whether a ModSec deny-event, APF block, YARA/LMD signature hit, or anomaly flag is a genuine threat signal or a benign-pattern match warranting suppression.

Use when the curator must decide whether to act on an alert or suppress it: applying the counter-hypothesis discipline, checking the benign-pattern corpus, verifying scanner thresholds, and emitting a suppression rationale with evidence citations to defense-hits.md when the FP-gate fires.

Prefer prescribing-defensive-payloads when the goal is to AUTHOR new rules. Prefer synthesizing-evidence when the goal is cross-stream timeline reconstruction, not alert adjudication.

Examples: "ModSec rule 981172 fired on a Magento admin POST — is this a scanner FP or a real credential-stuffing attempt?" / "APF blocked 93.184.216.34 — benign CDN IP, suppress and add to allowlist" / "YARA sig matched vendor/magento/framework/View/Asset/Bundle.php — known-good file, log suppression rationale."
